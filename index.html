From Faces to Voices: Learning Hierarchical Representations for High-quality Video-to-Speech

Anonymous submission


All source code and checkpoints will be released once the paper is accepted.

Abstract

The goal of this paper is to reconstruct high-quality speech from silent talking face videos, a task also known as video-to-speech synthesis. A significant challenge in video-to-speech synthesis lies in the modality gap between silent video and human speech that comprises a mixture of various attributes such as timbre, content, and pitch. To address this, we propose a high-quality video-to-speech system that effectively bridges this substantial modality gap through the learning of hierarchical associations between video and speech. Specifically, we introduce a dual-path visual frontend that extracts multi-faceted visual representations by comprehensively analyzing both lip movements and full-face sequences. Furthermore, we present a hierarchical encoder that progressively aligns these visual representations with various speech attributes, starting from a simple unimodal attribute and advancing to complex multimodal features. Finally, we employ a flow matching model to generate realistic speech without compromising speech details. Extensive experiments using a range of evaluation metrics demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms previous approaches, even surpassing the naturalness of real utterances in objective evaluations.
